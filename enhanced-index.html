Building Physical Literacy Through Movement

Welcome to The Birches Academy's comprehensive K-8 Physical Education program in Salem, NH. Our curriculum aligns with the 2024 SHAPE America National Standards and New Hampshire state requirements, serving 14-24 students per class across three unique learning environments: The Woods (café), outdoor recess hardtop, and classroom spaces. Each 50-minute weekly session is designed to develop confident, competent movers who embrace lifelong physical activity.

New Hampshire PE Standards Alignment

Our curriculum meets and exceeds all state and national standards for physical education

Standard 1

Motor Skills Development

Students develop a variety of motor skills across multiple environments, progressing from basic locomotor movements to sport-specific techniques.

K-2: Basic movements 3-5: Skill combinations 6-8: Sport applications
Standard 2

Movement & Fitness Concepts

Students apply knowledge of movement concepts, tactics, and strategies while understanding health and skill-related fitness principles.

K-2: Space awareness 3-5: Game strategies 6-8: Fitness planning
Standard 3

Social Skills Through Movement

Students develop empathy, respect, communication, and leadership skills through varied physical activity settings.

K-2: Cooperation 3-5: Teamwork 6-8: Leadership
Standard 4

Personal Benefits & Engagement

Students identify personal benefits of movement and choose to engage in meaningful physical activities for lifelong wellness.

K-2: Joy in movement 3-5: Goal setting 6-8: Personal fitness

Essential Routines for Every Class

Dynamic Warm-Up (5-7 minutes)

K-2 Warm-Up Sequence:

  1. Circle Time (1 min): Gather, greet, preview activities
  2. Animal Walks (2 min): Bear crawl, crab walk, frog jumps
  3. Follow the Leader (2 min): Various locomotor movements
  4. Stretching Story (2 min): Narrative-based stretches

3-5 Warm-Up Sequence:

  1. Jogging Variations (2 min): Forward, backward, lateral
  2. Dynamic Stretches (2 min): Leg swings, arm circles, lunges
  3. Sport-Specific Movements (2 min): Related to day's activity
  4. Heart Rate Check (1 min): Find pulse, count beats

6-8 Warm-Up Sequence:

  1. Cardio Progression (2 min): Walk to jog to run
  2. Dynamic Flexibility (3 min): Full-body movement patterns
  3. Activation Exercises (2 min): Core, glutes, shoulders
  4. Mental Preparation (30 sec): Focus and goal setting

Cool-Down & Recovery (5-7 minutes)

K-2 Cool-Down Sequence:

  1. Slow Motion Walk (1 min): Gradually decrease pace
  2. Breathing Exercises (2 min): Balloon breathing, counting breaths
  3. Partner Stretches (2 min): Mirror stretching with classmates
  4. Reflection Circle (2 min): Share favorite moments

3-5 Cool-Down Sequence:

  1. Walking Lap (1 min): Controlled breathing focus
  2. Static Stretching (3 min): Major muscle groups, 15-20 seconds each
  3. Mindfulness Moment (1 min): Body scan or visualization
  4. Exit Ticket (2 min): Quick skill review or question

6-8 Cool-Down Sequence:

  1. Recovery Walk (1 min): Heart rate reduction
  2. Flexibility Training (3 min): Sport-specific stretches
  3. Foam Rolling (2 min): When equipment available
  4. Goal Reflection (1 min): Personal progress check

Location-Specific Activity Examples

The Woods (Café) Activities

  • K-2: Movement stations with yoga mats, balance beams made from tape
  • 3-5: Fitness circuits using body weight, resistance bands
  • 6-8: Dance units, mindfulness sessions, health education

Outside (Hardtop) Activities

  • K-2: Chalk obstacle courses, hopscotch variations, tag games
  • 3-5: Four square, basketball skills, track events
  • 6-8: Team sports, fitness testing, ultimate frisbee

Classroom Activities

  • K-2: Brain breaks, desk yoga, movement stories
  • 3-5: Fitness videos, health lessons, sport education model
  • 6-8: Fitness planning, nutrition education, mental health
